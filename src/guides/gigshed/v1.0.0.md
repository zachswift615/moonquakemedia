## 1. Welcome to GigShed

GigShed helps you **learn a song before you perform it**. You bring in a recording you already own, and GigShed pulls it apart into its individual instruments, lets you slow it down or change its key, mutes or solos any part so you can play along, and builds a **chords-and-lyrics chart** you can fix up while the audio plays in your ears. When the chart is ready, it flows straight into its companion performance app, **GigScroll**, for the stage.

The name is a nod to *"woodshedding"* — musician slang for shutting yourself away to practice.

<div class="feature-box"><h4>{% icon "shield", "inline-icon" %} On-device, offline, private</h4><p>Everything happens <strong>on your device</strong>. Your recordings are never uploaded. There is no account, no subscription, and no internet required — GigShed works on a plane or in a venue with bad Wi-Fi. GigShed is a <strong>one-time purchase</strong>, not a rental.</p></div>

### What you can do

- **Separate** any song into four stems — drums, bass, vocals, and "other" (guitars/keys/etc.).
- **Mix** those stems — mute, solo, and set the volume of each part.
- **Re-key and slow down** — change the key in semitones and the speed independently, in real time.
- **Loop and drill** any section, with markers that snap cleanly to bars and beats.
- **Follow a beat grid** with a beat-locked metronome and count-in.
- **Build a chord chart** — either by pasting the real lyrics (best results) or fully automatically.
- **Edit the chart** with the exact audio for each line one tap away.
- **Export** your processed mix, export the chart as ChordPro or PDF, or send it to GigScroll.

<div class="tip-box"><h4>{% icon "flask-conical", "inline-icon" %} You're using the beta</h4><p>GigShed is currently in <strong>public beta on TestFlight</strong>. Features are still settling and you may hit rough edges — please keep backups of anything important, and send feedback so we can make it better. <a href="https://testflight.apple.com/join/7gtu3U2T">Join or manage the beta on TestFlight &rarr;</a></p></div>

<div class="feature-box"><h4>{% icon "circle-alert", "inline-icon" %} Bring your own music</h4><p>GigShed has no streaming or downloader — it only works on audio files <strong>you already have</strong>. Use music you've purchased and downloaded, ripped from your own CDs, or recorded yourself. You're responsible for having the right to use whatever you import.</p></div>

## 2. Importing Songs & the Library

### 2.1 The Library

When you open GigShed you land on the **Library** — your list of songs. Each song is a **Project** that remembers everything you've done to it: its stems, your mix, your key and speed, your loops, your beat grid, and your chart.

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/library.png" alt="The GigShed Library screen listing songs, each showing stem and chart status">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">The Library. Each row shows the song's status — whether stems and a chart exist — and any key/speed you've dialled in (for example, <span class="button-label">+4 st · 1.5×</span>).</figcaption>
</figure>

At the top of the Library are a **gear** (Settings) on the left, a **+** (add a song) on the right, a **Search** field, and your songs grouped by status (for example, **In Progress**). Each row shows:

- the song **title**,
- a **status line** such as *"Stems ✓ · no chart"* or *"Stems ✓ · Chart draft"*, and
- if you've changed the key or speed, a gold summary like *"+4 st · 1.5×"*.

Tap a row to open that song's **workbench**.

### 2.2 Adding a Song

Tap **+** in the top-right to open the import menu:

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/import-menu.png" alt="The import menu with three options: Import audio (Files), Import from Music Library, and Import .gigshed bundle">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">Three ways to bring in a song.</figcaption>
</figure>

| Option | What it does |
|---|---|
| <span class="button-label">Import audio (Files)</span> | Pick an audio file from the Files app (or a connected drive). GigShed reads **mp3, wav, aiff, m4a/aac, and flac**. |
| <span class="button-label">Import from Music Library</span> | Choose from music stored in your device's Music library. *(DRM-protected Apple Music streaming tracks can't be used — only files you actually own.)* |
| <span class="button-label">Import .gigshed bundle</span> | Open a project someone shared, or one you exported from another device. |

The imported file is **copied into the project**, so GigShed keeps working even if the original file moves or is deleted.

<div class="tip-box"><h4>{% icon "lightbulb", "inline-icon" %} The 5-song free limit</h4><p>The free version of GigShed holds <strong>5 songs</strong>. Every feature works on all five — nothing is crippled. To keep more than five songs at once, unlock <strong>GigShed Pro</strong>, a one-time purchase (see <a href="#pro">GigShed Pro &amp; Settings</a>).</p></div>

## 3. The Workbench — A Tour

Opening a song takes you to its **workbench**, where all the practice tools live. The layout is the same for every song:

- **Top bar** — a back chevron (to the Library), the song title, and a **•••** (more) button on the right for song actions like **Export**.
- **Tabs** — <span class="button-label">Mix</span>, <span class="button-label">Tune</span>, <span class="button-label">Loop</span>, and <span class="button-label">Chart</span>. Each tab is one family of tools; switching tabs never stops playback.
- **Transport bar** — pinned to the bottom on every tab.

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/mix.png" alt="The workbench showing the Mix tab, the four tabs across the top, and the transport bar at the bottom">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">The workbench, here on the Mix tab. The four tabs sit under the title; the transport bar runs along the bottom.</figcaption>
</figure>

### The transport bar

The transport bar is always available while you work:

- a **scrubber** you can drag to move through the song,
- **elapsed** and **total** time,
- **rewind**, **play/pause**, and **fast-forward** buttons,
- a live readout of the **lyric word** and **chord** at the current position, and
- a **loop** toggle (it turns gold when a loop is active).

<div class="feature-box"><h4>{% icon "clock", "inline-icon" %} One timeline, always in sync</h4><p>Everything in GigShed is pinned to the song's original timeline. When you change speed or key, the beat grid, chords, loops, and lyric timings all stay lined up — you're changing how the song <em>sounds</em>, never where things happen in it.</p></div>

## 4. Separating & Mixing Stems

### 4.1 Separating into Stems

Stem separation is the engine behind almost everything else. **One tap** splits the song into four tracks — **drums, bass, vocals,** and **other** (guitars, keys, and everything that isn't the first three).

Separation runs **on your device** and takes a little while (longer songs and older devices take more time). You only do it once per song; the result is saved with the project. While it runs, GigShed is busy with that one job — see the note about the model engine in [Troubleshooting](#troubleshooting).

### 4.2 The Stem Mixer (Mix tab)

The **Mix** tab is the heart of practice. Each stem has a volume slider and two round buttons:

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/mix.png" alt="The Mix tab with Drums, Bass, Other, and Vocals, each with solo and mute buttons and a volume slider, plus preset buttons">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">Drums, Bass, Other, and Vocals — each with Solo (S), Mute (M), and a volume slider. Presets are one-tap mixes.</figcaption>
</figure>

- **S — Solo.** Hear *only* this stem (a filled S means solo is on). Solo more than one to hear just those parts together.
- **M — Mute.** Silence this stem.
- **Volume slider** — set each part's level in the mix.

Below the sliders are one-tap **Presets**:

| Preset | What you hear |
|---|---|
| <span class="button-label">Kill vocals</span> | The full band with the lead vocal muted — sing it yourself. |
| <span class="button-label">Band only</span> | Everything except vocals. |
| <span class="button-label">Solo bass</span> | Just the bass, to lock in a line. |
| <span class="button-label">Just drums</span> | Only the drums, to play along to the groove. |

<div class="tip-box"><h4>{% icon "lightbulb", "inline-icon" %} Solo vs. Mute</h4><p>Reach for <strong>Solo</strong> when you want to study one part in isolation (learn the bass line), and <strong>Mute</strong> when you want to remove your own part and play along with everyone else (mute the guitar and cover it live).</p></div>

</div>

## 5. Changing Key & Speed

The **Tune** tab changes the song's **key** and **speed** independently and in real time — without the "chipmunk" or "underwater" artifacts you get from naïve pitch/speed changes.

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/tune.png" alt="The Tune tab showing a Key stepper at minus 4 semitones (C to G sharp) and a Speed control at 0.8x with preset buttons">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">Key and Speed are separate controls. Here the song is down 4 semitones and playing at 0.8×.</figcaption>
</figure>

### 5.1 Key

Use the **−** / **+** stepper under **Key** to move the song by semitones. The readout shows the shift and where it lands — for example, *−4 st* with *C → G#*. Lower it to fit your voice, or raise it to match a capo position.

### 5.2 Speed

Drag the **Speed** slider, or tap a preset: <span class="button-label">¼×</span>, <span class="button-label">½×</span>, <span class="button-label">1×</span>, <span class="button-label">1½×</span>, <span class="button-label">2×</span>. Slow a tricky lick right down to learn it, then bring it back up as you get it. The pitch stays correct at every speed.

### 5.3 Reset

Tap <span class="button-label">Reset to original</span> to return to the original key and full speed in one move.

<div class="feature-box"><h4>{% icon "music", "inline-icon" %} Key and speed are independent</h4><p>Changing the key never changes the tempo, and changing the speed never changes the pitch. Slow a solo to half speed in its original key, or transpose down a few semitones at full tempo — whatever helps you learn it.</p></div>

<div class="tip-box"><h4>{% icon "lightbulb", "inline-icon" %} Your settings are remembered</h4><p>Your key and speed are saved with the song and shown on its Library row (for example, <span class="button-label">+4 st · 1.5×</span>). If you also export audio, the export is rendered <em>with</em> your current key and speed baked in.</p></div>

</div>

## 6. Looping & Section Practice

The **Loop** tab lets you repeat a section over and over — the single most useful thing you can do while learning a passage. A loop is defined by a start point (**A**) and an end point (**B**); GigShed repeats everything between them, gaplessly.

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/loop.png" alt="The Loop tab showing the A/B loop card and the Beat Grid card below it">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">The Loop tab: the A–B loop card on top, the Beat Grid card below.</figcaption>
</figure>

### 6.1 Choosing how points snap

Before you set a loop, pick a **Snap** mode:

| Snap | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| <span class="button-label">Bar</span> | Set A / Set B snap to the nearest bar line (downbeat). *Default.* |
| <span class="button-label">Beat</span> | Set A / Set B snap to the nearest beat. |
| <span class="button-label">Off</span> | Set A / Set B land exactly at the playhead, with no snapping. |

Bar and beat snapping rely on the beat grid, so [detect a grid](#grid) first for musical loops. With snapping on, you don't have to be precise — tap roughly where you want the edge and GigShed lands it on the nearest bar or beat.

### 6.2 Setting a loop by hand

1. Move the playhead to where the loop should start (drag the scrubber).
2. Tap <span class="button-label">Set A</span>.
3. Move to where it should end and tap <span class="button-label">Set B</span>.

The status line shows the loop's start, end, and length with a *⟳ on* indicator. The loop is active immediately — press play and it repeats. Tap <span class="button-label">Clear</span> to remove it.

### 6.3 Looping a set number of bars

When a beat grid exists, two shortcuts appear: <span class="button-label">Loop 4 bars</span> and <span class="button-label">Loop 8 bars</span>. Each creates a loop that starts at A (snapped to the nearest bar) and spans exactly that many bars. If you haven't set A yet, the loop starts at the bar the playhead is currently in — so you can drop into a song and instantly drill a phrase.

<div class="feature-box"><h4>{% icon "repeat", "inline-icon" %} Seamless loops</h4><p>GigShed's loops are gapless. When playback reaches B it returns to A with no audible stutter, and the metronome click stays perfectly in time across the seam — so a 4-bar loop feels like the section was recorded on an endless tape.</p></div>

</div>

## 7. Beat Grid, Metronome & Count-In

A **beat grid** is GigShed's map of the song's pulse — the time of every beat and downbeat. It's optional, but once you have one you unlock bar/beat snapping (above), a **beat-locked metronome**, and a **count-in**. The Beat Grid tools live on the lower half of the **Loop** tab.

### 7.1 What the beat grid is

The grid is a **tempo map**, not a single fixed tempo. GigShed detects each individual beat, so a song that speeds up or breathes is represented faithfully. The tempo number you see (for example, *♩ = 84*) is a convenient **average** for display — the real information is the position of each beat.

A grid carries a **source**. When GigShed finds it automatically it's labelled **Detected**; as soon as you correct it by hand it becomes **Manual**. The readout always tells you which you're looking at, along with the meter and how many beats and bars it contains (for example, *Detected grid · 289 beats · 73 bars*).

### 7.2 Detecting the grid

On the **Loop** tab, find the **Beat Grid** card and tap <span class="button-label">Analyze grid</span>. GigShed studies the full mix (you don't need to solo or mute anything). When it finishes, the card shows the readout plus <span class="button-label">Re-analyze</span> and <span class="button-label">Edit grid</span>.

<div class="feature-box"><h4>{% icon "target", "inline-icon" %} No fabricated grids</h4><p>Some recordings — free-time intros, rubato ballads — have no steady pulse. Rather than invent a grid it doesn't believe in, GigShed tells you honestly and offers <strong>tap-tempo</strong> instead: tap along a few times, choose the beats per bar, and it lays down a steady manual grid from your taps.</p></div>

### 7.3 The Grid Editor

Tap <span class="button-label">Edit grid</span> to open the full-screen Grid Editor. At the top is a scrollable strip showing your grid over time — **tall lines** are bar lines (numbered), **short lines** are beats, and a **playhead** tracks playback. A transport bar is pinned at the bottom so you can audition the grid without leaving. The editor gives you four whole-grid corrections, each of which turns the grid **Manual**:

- **Set bar 1** — tap the beat that should be counted as "one." The bar lines re-phase; the beats don't move.
- **Half-time / Double-time** — if the felt tempo is twice or half what you count, halve or double it.
- **Nudge (−10 ms / +10 ms)** — slide the *whole* grid a hair earlier or later so the click sits exactly on the drums.
- **Re-detect beats** — run automatic detection again from scratch.

<div class="warning-box"><h4>{% icon "triangle-alert", "inline-icon" %} Correcting the grid never moves the audio</h4><p>Every correction slides the <em>grid lines</em> against the recording — the recording itself never moves. You can experiment freely; you're never editing or damaging the song. (Re-detecting a grid you corrected by hand <em>will</em> replace your manual work, so GigShed asks you to confirm first.)</p></div>

### 7.4 Metronome & Count-In

Once a grid exists, the Beat Grid card adds:

- **Metronome** toggle — a click locked to the grid. It follows tempo drift, accents the downbeat, and plays on its own channel (audible even with stems muted or soloed).
- **Count-in** — <span class="button-label">Off</span>, <span class="button-label">1 bar</span>, or <span class="button-label">2 bars</span> of clicks before playback so you can come in on time.
- **Auto-analyze after separation** — **off by default.** Turn it on if you'd like a grid to be ready automatically each time a new song finishes separating.

</div>

## 8. Chord & Lyric Charts

GigShed can build a **chords-over-lyrics chart** in the standard ChordPro format. This is the app's headline feature — and the one place where it's important to set your expectations. There are **two paths** to a chart, and *which one you choose makes a big difference to the result.*

<div class="warning-box"><h4>{% icon "triangle-alert", "inline-icon" %} Read this before you generate a chart</h4><p><strong>Automatic lyric transcription is the weakest link.</strong> Machines are not good at hearing sung words — especially melodic, drawn-out, or heavily-produced choruses. The fully-automatic path will get plenty of words wrong. <strong>If you know the lyrics — or can find them — paste them in.</strong> Pasting the real words is dramatically more accurate and is the experience GigShed is built around. Treat auto-transcription as "better than a blank page," not as a finished chart.</p></div>

### 8.1 Path A — Paste the lyrics (recommended)

Give GigShed the **real lyrics** and it stops guessing the words entirely. It aligns each word you pasted to the vocal in the recording and places the detected chords above them.

For the best possible result, **paste or type the lyrics exactly as they are sung**, including:

- **Repeats.** If the singer repeats *"never gonna give you up"* four times, write it four times. Alignment matches what it hears against what you typed — so the words on the page should mirror what's actually sung, line for line.
- **Ad-libs and sung filler** — *"yeah,"* *"oh,"* *"na na na"* — if you can hear it, type it.
- **Line breaks** where the phrases break.

You can optionally mark sections with headers like `[Chorus]` or `[Verse 1]` (see [Editing](#editing)); GigShed will keep them.

<div class="tip-box"><h4>{% icon "lightbulb", "inline-icon" %} Where to get accurate lyrics</h4><p>Lyrics sites are a good start, but they're often not written the way the song is actually <em>performed</em> — they leave out repeats and ad-libs and sometimes reorder sections. Paste them in, then <strong>listen through once and fix the words to match what you hear.</strong> Ten minutes of this yields a chart worlds better than anything automatic.</p></div>

### 8.2 Path B — Fully automatic

If you don't have the lyrics, GigShed will transcribe the vocal itself, work out the song's structure, detect the chords, and assemble the whole chart with **zero input** from you. It's the lowest-effort path — and it inherits every mistake the automatic transcription makes.

Expect to correct it. Spoken-word or clear, front-of-mix vocals fare best; melodic and melismatic choruses fare worst. This is a genuine limitation of off-the-shelf speech recognition on singing, not a setting you can tune away.

### 8.3 Building (or rebuilding) the chart

The **Chart** tab is where charts are created and regenerated. When a chart exists, its **Regenerate** section offers:

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/chart-tab.png" alt="The Chart tab showing a chord chart preview and a Regenerate section with Auto-transcribe lyrics, Re-paste lyrics and align, and Re-detect chords">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">The Chart tab. Tap the preview to open the full editor; use Regenerate to rebuild lyrics or chords.</figcaption>
</figure>

- <span class="button-label">Auto-transcribe lyrics</span> — (re)build the chart the fully-automatic way (Path A above is the paste alternative).
- <span class="button-label">Re-paste lyrics & align</span> — paste corrected or complete lyrics and re-align them. **This is the path to reach for.**
- <span class="button-label">Re-detect chords</span> — re-run chord detection only, leaving your lyrics alone.

Tap the chart preview (*"Tap to open the full editor"*) to jump into the editor.

<div class="feature-box"><h4>{% icon "sparkles", "inline-icon" %} Draft, then correct</h4><p>The philosophy behind GigShed's charts is "draft, then correct." No auto-generated chart is finished — the app's real job is to make <em>fixing</em> it fast, with the exact audio for every line one tap away. That's what the editor is for.</p></div>

</div>

## 9. Editing Your Chart

Open the editor by tapping the chart preview on the Chart tab. The editor title reads *"Song · Chart"* and has a **Save** button, a **•••** menu, and a segmented switch between two views: **Chart** (the pretty rendered view) and **Source** (the raw ChordPro text).

### 9.1 The Chart (rendered) view

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/chart-view.png" alt="The rendered chart view with section headers, gold chord chips over the lyrics, a transpose bar, and a Following Tune toggle">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">The rendered chart: chords sit as gold chips above the words, grouped into sections. The transpose bar sits at the top.</figcaption>
</figure>

Chords appear as gold chips above the lyrics, grouped into sections like *[Verse 1]* and *[Pre-chorus]*. Playback highlights your place, and the transport bar works exactly as it does elsewhere.

**Fixing a chord.** Tap a chord chip to select it. A small toolbar appears with tools for that chord — **hear/loop** just that chord's slice of audio, **edit** the chord text, **retime** where it sits, **Detect from notes** (see below), and **delete** it. Because you can loop and solo the exact moment a chord plays, you can *check it by ear* and fix it on the spot.

<div class="tip-box"><h4>{% icon "lightbulb", "inline-icon" %} Tap-to-delete a wrong chord</h4><p>Detected too many chords? Select the offending chip and tap the trash tool to remove it. Nothing else shifts — the surrounding chords and lyrics stay put.</p></div>

### 9.2 Transpose & "Following Tune"

The bar at the top of the rendered view transposes the **chart** in semitones with **−** / **+**. A <span class="button-label">Following Tune</span> toggle links the chart to the **Tune** tab's key change:

- **Following Tune (on)** — the chart automatically shows chords in whatever key you've set on the Tune tab, so the paper always matches what you're hearing.
- **Following Tune (off)** — the chart uses its own independent transpose, which you set here — handy when you want to *play* in an easy shape while the audio stays in the original key.

### 9.3 The Source (ChordPro) view

Switch to **Source** to edit the chart as text. GigShed uses **ChordPro**: chords go in square brackets right before the syllable they land on — `I can play that for you on [C]my pink gu[C#]itar` — and sections are wrapped in `{start_of_verse: Verse 1}` … `{end_of_verse}` directives.

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/chart-source.png" alt="The Source view showing raw ChordPro text with a keyboard accessory bar offering ChordPro and Section helpers">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">The Source view. The accessory bar above the keyboard gives you ChordPro and Section helpers so you don't have to type directives by hand.</figcaption>
</figure>

You rarely need to memorize the syntax — the accessory bar above the keyboard does it for you:

<div class="guide-figure-row">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/chart-keyboard-menu.png" alt="The ChordPro helper menu with Set Capo, Set Key, Insert Comment, and Insert Chord">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/chart-section-menu.png" alt="The Section menu with Custom, Solo, Outro, Intro, Bridge, Pre-Chorus, Chorus, and Verse">
</div>

- The <span class="button-label">{ } ChordPro</span> menu inserts common directives — **Set Capo**, **Set Key**, **Insert Comment**, and **Insert Chord**.
- The <span class="button-label">Section</span> menu wraps a new section around your cursor — **Intro, Verse, Pre-Chorus, Chorus, Bridge, Solo, Outro,** or a **Custom** label.

Tap <span class="button-label">Save</span> when you're done. Anything you can do in Source shows up in the rendered Chart view, and vice versa — they're two views of the same chart.

</div>

## 10. Detect from Notes

Sometimes the automatic chord detector is unsure — a dense voicing, a passing chord, an unusual extension. **Detect from Notes** is a second opinion: GigShed transcribes the **actual notes ringing out** at that moment and offers the chords those notes best spell, so you can pick the right one.

Select a chord in the chart editor and open its tools, then choose the **note-detection** tool. A sheet appears:

<figure class="guide-figure">
  <img class="guide-screenshot" src="/images/guides/gigshed/detect-from-notes.png" alt="The Detect from Notes sheet listing candidate chords with confidence percentages and a list of detected notes with toggles">
  <figcaption class="guide-figcaption">Candidate chords (with confidence) up top; the notes GigShed heard, each with a toggle, below.</figcaption>
</figure>

- **Chords** — a ranked list of candidates with a confidence percentage (for example, *C6/G — 77%*). Tap one to use it.
- **Detected notes** — every note GigShed heard, each with a toggle. **Turn a note off** to tell GigShed to ignore it (say it picked up bleed from another instrument); the chord candidates above **re-rank instantly** from the notes that remain.

<div class="tip-box"><h4>{% icon "lightbulb", "inline-icon" %} About the bass note</h4><p>The lowest note sets the "slash" in a slash chord (the <em>/G</em> in <em>C6/G</em>). The detected bass note isn't editable directly — if you don't want a slash chord, just pick one of the non-slash candidates from the list.</p></div>

</div>

## 13. How-To — Common Tasks

Quick recipes for the things people ask for most.

### Change the key of a song
1. Open the song and go to the **Tune** tab.
2. Under **Key**, tap **−** or **+** to move by semitones until the *→* readout shows your target (e.g. *C → A*).
3. Press play. To also have the **chart** follow, open the chart editor and make sure <span class="button-label">Following Tune</span> is on.

### Slow a song down (to learn a hard part)
1. **Tune** tab → **Speed**.
2. Drag the slider or tap a preset like <span class="button-label">½×</span>.
3. Pair it with a loop (below) to drill the passage, and speed back up as you improve. The pitch stays correct at every speed.

### Mute the vocals (sing it yourself)
1. **Mix** tab.
2. Tap <span class="button-label">Kill vocals</span>, **or** tap the **M** next to **Vocals**.

### Mute the guitar / keys (play it yourself)
1. **Mix** tab.
2. Tap the **M** next to **Other** — guitars and keys live in the "other" stem.

### Mute the drums or bass
1. **Mix** tab.
2. Tap the **M** next to **Drums** or **Bass**. (For only drums, the <span class="button-label">Just drums</span> preset solos them instead.)

### Solo the bass (learn the line)
1. **Mix** tab.
2. Tap <span class="button-label">Solo bass</span>, **or** tap the **S** next to **Bass**.

### Loop a solo or tricky section
1. **Loop** tab. Set **Snap** to <span class="button-label">Bar</span>.
2. Scrub to the start and tap <span class="button-label">Set A</span>, then to the end and tap <span class="button-label">Set B</span> — or just tap <span class="button-label">Loop 8 bars</span> at the start.
3. Press play; it repeats seamlessly. Add the **Metronome** and a **1-bar count-in** for a clean practice bed.

### Get an accurate chord chart
1. Find the song's lyrics and **listen through once**, fixing them to match what's actually sung (repeats and all).
2. **Chart** tab → <span class="button-label">Re-paste lyrics & align</span> (or the paste option when creating the first chart) and paste them in.
3. Open the editor, play it back, and correct any chords by tapping each chip and checking it by ear. Use [Detect from Notes](#notes) on the stubborn ones.

### Send a finished chart to GigScroll
1. Finish the chart in GigShed.
2. **•••** menu → **Export** → <span class="button-label">Send to GigScroll</span>.
3. Open GigScroll — the song is there, ready for the stage.

## 14. Tips & Troubleshooting

### A control is greyed out while a song is separating
GigShed runs **one on-device model at a time** to stay within memory. While a song is separating (or another analysis is running), detection controls and some actions are dimmed. Wait for the current job to finish, then try again.

### Separation is taking a long time
Separation is the heaviest job in the app, and it runs entirely on your device. Longer songs and older devices take more time. Let it finish — it's a one-time step per song, and the result is saved.

### The automatic lyrics are full of mistakes
That's expected — automatic transcription of singing is the app's weakest link. Don't fight it: use <span class="button-label">Re-paste lyrics & align</span> and give GigShed the **real lyrics**, typed the way they're actually sung. See [Chord & Lyric Charts](#charts).

### A chord is wrong
Open the chart editor, tap the chord chip, and use its tools to hear/loop that exact moment, then edit or delete it. For a second opinion, use [Detect from Notes](#notes) and toggle off any notes that don't belong.

### The chords are in the wrong key on the page
In the chart editor, check the transpose bar and the <span class="button-label">Following Tune</span> toggle. With it **on**, the chart follows the Tune tab's key; with it **off**, it uses its own transpose that you set with **−** / **+**.

### The metronome click sounds slightly ahead of / behind the beat
Open the **Grid Editor**, turn the metronome on, and **Nudge** the grid (−10 ms / +10 ms) until the click sits on top of the drums. The audio never moves — only the grid.

### "Analyze grid" said it couldn't find a steady pulse
The track is likely free-time or very soft. Use the **tap-tempo** panel: tap the beat a few times, choose the beats per bar, and lay down a manual grid.

### I've hit the 5-song limit
Delete a song you're done with (swipe its Library row, or use the **•••** menu inside the song), or unlock **GigShed Pro** for unlimited songs (see [GigShed Pro & Settings](#pro)).

### An imported song won't load
GigShed needs an actual audio file you own (mp3, wav, aiff, m4a/aac, flac). DRM-protected streaming tracks (e.g. Apple Music) can't be used. Try exporting/purchasing a real file and importing that.

### I found a bug in the beta
Thank you — that's exactly what the beta is for. Note the **Version** shown in Settings and what you were doing, and send it our way at [support@moonquakemedia.com](mailto:support@moonquakemedia.com).
